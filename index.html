Cycling Buddy SG — your Singapore cycling companion

Cycling Buddy SG is a free, offline cycling map for Singapore — the whole network on one map: the 7 Park Connector loops, LTA's cycling paths, the Rail Corridor, every park and nature reserve, and bike parking island-wide. Plan a route offline, check the rain before you go, find your nearest connector, and record your ride — installable, free, and no app store.

How to use

  • Drag to pan, pinch/scroll to zoom, tap anything — a path, a park, a rack — to identify it.
  • ◎ Locate — live position + the nearest connector.
  • ➤ Compass — with GPS on, one tap zooms in and turns the map to face the way you're heading. Tap again for north-up. Twist with two fingers to rotate any time.
  • ⦚ Route — tap a start & destination for offline turn-by-turn that prefers park connectors.
  • ⛅ Weather — a live 2-hour rain forecast for your area (and along your planned route), with a heads-up when thundery showers are likely. Tap the cloud button (top bar) for a rain-zone map of the whole island — coloured zones are wet, everywhere else is clear to ride.
  • 🌳 Parks — every park & nature reserve is washed in green; tap one for its name and size. Toggle them in the Layers panel.
  • P Bike parking — zoom in past street level and LTA's bike racks appear. Solid P = sheltered, hollow = open-air; tap for the number of spaces. When you're located, the nearest rack shows in the panel — tap it to jump there.
  • ● Record — traces your ride with distance, time and speed; export GPX.
  • Toggle any layer — loops, Rail Corridor, cycling paths, parks, bike parking, diversions — in the Layers panel; tap its ⤢ to frame it.
  • ⚠️ Diversions — a red glow over part of a loop, with a caution marker, flags a stretch affected by a closure (e.g. Gardens by the Bay's Bay South waterfront during the bridge works; the detour runs around the perimeter). Tap it for the official diversion map, and follow on-site signage.
  • ⬇ Install — tap the install button (top bar) to add it to your home screen and run it offline like a native app.

Common questions

Is Cycling Buddy SG free?

Yes — completely free, with no ads, no sign-up and no tracking cookies. It's built and maintained by Lin Jiaen using open data from NParks, LTA and NEA via data.gov.sg.

Does it work offline?

Yes. It's a Progressive Web App: once loaded it caches the map, the routing graph and the network layers, so route planning and the park-connector map keep working without a connection. Only the live rain radar needs a connection to refresh.

What is the Park Connector Network (PCN)?

Singapore's islandwide system of cycling and walking paths — about 334.8 km across 7 colour-coded loops — linking parks, nature reserves and neighbourhoods. This app maps all 7 loops and plans routes that prefer them over roads.

How do I install it on my phone?

Open the site in your phone's browser and choose Add to Home Screen — the Share menu in Safari on iPhone, or the browser menu in Chrome on Android. It then runs full-screen and offline like a native app.

Do I need a helmet?

Helmets are required by law when cycling on Singapore roads. The app prefers park connectors and avoids expressways, and shows a helmet reminder whenever a route has to use roads.

Add to Home Screen

On iPhone or iPad, in Safari:

  1. Tap the Share button (the square with an up-arrow) in the toolbar.
  2. Scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen.
  3. Tap Add — it installs as Cycling Buddy and opens full-screen.

Add to Home Screen only appears in Safari, not Chrome, on iOS.

In your browser's menu (⋮ or ⋯):

  1. Tap Install app or Add to Home Screen.
  2. Confirm — it installs as Cycling Buddy and works offline.

Don't see it yet? Pan the map for a few seconds, then try again — the browser needs a moment to qualify the app.
